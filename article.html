Unlocking the Secrets of Deep-Sea Coral Resilience

March 15, 2026 Research Update

A new study led by OIST researchers reveals the genetic mechanisms that allow certain coral species to thrive in extreme pressure and low light conditions.

Deep-sea corals are among the most mysterious organisms on our planet. Living thousands of meters below the surface, they face challenges that would be fatal to most life forms: crushing pressure, near-freezing temperatures, and a total absence of sunlight.

Adaptive Evolution

By sequencing the genomes of several species collected from the Ryukyu Trench, the team identified a suite of genes related to cell membrane stability and metabolic efficiency that appear to have undergone rapid evolution.

“These findings not only help us understand the history of life in our oceans but also provide insights into how organisms might adapt to changing environments in the future,” says Dr. Sarah Jenkins, lead author of the study.
